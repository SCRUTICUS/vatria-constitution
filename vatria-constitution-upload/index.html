VATRIAN CONSTITUTION

Articles

Article I. The Federation

A. "The Vatrian Federation"

I. Vatria, officially the Vatrian Federation, is a federal nation made up of equal recognized provinces.

II. The Federation shall be governed by an elected President, an elected Vice President, and the Vatrian Council.

III. The President leads the national government, the Council represents the provinces, and this Constitution stands above both.

IV. No citizen, province, official, or government body may hold authority above this Constitution.

B. "Purpose of Vatria"

I. The purpose of Vatria is to protect its citizens, preserve its provinces, maintain order, defend its territory, and promote cooperation between all parts of the Federation.

II. The Federation shall act in the common interest of Vatria while respecting the identity and local authority of each province.

III. The government of Vatria shall serve the Federation, not rule above it.

C. "Federal Unity"

I. All recognized provinces are equal members of the Federation.

II. No province shall receive greater federal power because of size, wealth, age, population, military strength, or importance.

III. The Federation may not remove the rights, identity, or territory of a province except by lawful process.

Article II. Citizenship, Activity, and Voting

A. "Citizenship"

I. A citizen of Vatria is any person officially recognized as a member of the Vatrian Federation.

II. Citizens may participate in national politics according to this Constitution and federal law.

III. Citizenship may not be removed secretly, unfairly, or for personal reasons.

B. "Active Status"

I. A citizen is active if they have joined the server within the last two months.

II. A citizen who has not joined the server for two months is inactive until they return.

III. Inactive citizens keep their citizenship but temporarily lose voting rights.

IV. A citizen, official, or Council member may avoid inactive status by publicly declaring a temporary absence.

C. "Voting Rights"

I. Only active citizens may vote in presidential elections.

II. Only active Council members may vote in Council matters.

III. Voting totals shall be based on active eligible voters, not on all historical citizens or former officials.

IV. Each active citizen has one vote in presidential elections.

V. No person may vote using another person’s account, name, or authority.

Article III. Provinces and the Council

A. "Recognized Provinces"

I. A province is a territory, city, region, or settlement officially recognized as part of Vatria.

II. A province must be recognized by the Council before receiving a Council seat.

III. A province may not be created only to gain extra political power.

B. "Council Membership"

I. The Vatrian Council is made up of the leaders or official representatives of recognized provinces.

II. Each recognized province receives one Council seat.

III. Each Council seat has one vote.

IV. A person may not hold more than one Council vote, even if they lead multiple provinces.

V. If a province has multiple leaders, it must choose one official Council representative.

C. "Powers of the Council"

I. The Council may pass federal laws.

II. The Council may approve or reject major presidential decisions.

III. The Council may veto presidential decisions.

IV. The Council may recognize provinces, approve treaties, approve wars, review emergencies, and amend this Constitution.

V. The Council may investigate serious misconduct by federal officials.

D. "Limits of the Council"

I. The Council may not cancel presidential elections.

II. The Council may not remove the President except through succession, resignation, inactivity, or impeachment.

III. The Council may not give one province more Council votes than another.

IV. The Council may not punish a citizen, official, or province only for disagreeing with the majority.

Article IV. The Presidency

A. "The President"

I. The President is the head of government of the Vatrian Federation.

II. The President represents Vatria in national, foreign, diplomatic, and public matters.

III. The President is responsible for leading the Federation, carrying out federal law, and protecting the interests of Vatria.

IV. The President may also be a Council member if they lead a recognized province, but they still receive only one Council vote.

B. "The Vice President"

I. The Vice President assists the President and acts as the second-highest executive official of Vatria.

II. The Vice President may represent Vatria when the President is absent or unable to act.

III. If the Presidency becomes vacant, the Vice President becomes President.

IV. The Vice President is bound by the same constitutional limits as the President.

C. "Presidential Authority"

I. The President may make ordinary executive decisions needed for the daily operation of Vatria.

II. Ordinary executive decisions include announcements, organization, project coordination, minor diplomacy, and temporary appointments.

III. The President may propose laws, treaties, alliances, wars, national projects, and constitutional amendments.

IV. Major national decisions require Council approval before becoming official.

D. "Limits of Presidential Power"

I. The President may not change this Constitution by personal order.

II. The President may not cancel, delay, or control presidential elections.

III. The President may not ignore a lawful Council veto.

IV. The President may not declare war, give away territory, expel provinces, create binding alliances, or make major foreign commitments without Council approval.

V. The President may not punish citizens, officials, or provinces for lawful disagreement.

Article V. Laws, Decisions, and Vetoes

A. "Types of Decisions"

I. Ordinary executive decisions may be made by the President without prior Council approval.

II. Major national decisions require approval from more than half of active Council votes.

III. Urgent decisions may be made immediately by the President if waiting would seriously harm Vatria, but they must be reviewed by the Council afterward.

IV. Constitutional amendments require at least 60% support from active Council votes.

B. "Council Laws"

I. The Council may pass ordinary federal laws with more than half of active Council votes.

II. Federal laws apply to all of Vatria unless the law states otherwise.

III. A Council law may not contradict this Constitution.

C. "Council Vetoes"

I. The Council may veto a presidential decision if at least 40% of active Council votes oppose it.

II. A veto cancels the presidential decision and prevents it from being enforced.

III. The President may propose a revised version of a vetoed decision.

IV. Ignoring a lawful veto is abuse of power.

D. "Voting Procedure"

I. Council votes may be held by Discord poll, written vote, in-game meeting, or another recognized method.

II. Votes must be clear, recorded, and reasonably accessible to active Council members.

III. Ordinary Council votes should remain open for at least 24 hours unless the matter is urgent.

IV. Constitutional amendment votes should remain open for at least 48 hours.

V. A tied vote fails unless this Constitution or federal law states otherwise.

Article VI. Elections, Terms, and Succession

A. "Presidential Elections"

I. Presidential elections shall be held every December.

II. The election shall choose both a President and a Vice President.

III. Presidential and vice-presidential candidates shall run together as one ticket.

IV. All active citizens may vote.

V. The ticket with the most valid votes wins.

B. "Election Rules"

I. Each active citizen has one vote.

II. No person may vote more than once.

III. No person may vote using another person’s identity.

IV. No candidate or official may use threats, bribery, fraud, or vote manipulation.

V. If two or more tickets tie for first place, a runoff election shall be held between the tied tickets.

C. "Term of Office"

I. The President and Vice President serve until the next elected President and Vice President take office.

II. No President or Vice President may cancel the December election.

III. A President or Vice President may resign by publicly announcing their resignation.

D. "Succession"

I. If the President resigns, is removed, leaves Vatria, or abandons office, the Vice President becomes President.

II. If both the President and Vice President are unable to serve, the Council shall choose an Acting President.

III. If more than two months remain before the next December election, the Council may call a special election.

IV. If less than two months remain before the next December election, the Acting President may finish the term.

E. "Abandonment of Office"

I. The President or Vice President abandons office if they do not join the server for two months without an official absence.

II. Abandonment of office must be confirmed by the Council.

III. A former official who returns after abandonment does not automatically regain office.

Article VII. Misconduct, Leaks, and Impeachment

A. "Official Misconduct"

I. Officials of Vatria must act in good faith and use their power for the Federation, not for personal control.

II. Abuse of power, corruption, sabotage, election fraud, treason, serious dishonesty, or violation of this Constitution may be punished by law.

III. No official may create fake provinces, fake votes, fake emergencies, or fake inactivity to gain power.

B. "Information and Leaks"

I. Government information may be public, internal, confidential, or secret.

II. Leaking confidential or secret information without permission is a serious offense.

III. Officials may not share private government information with foreign nations, enemies, or unauthorized people.

IV. A person may report corruption, abuse of power, or unconstitutional actions to the Council without being treated as a leaker.

V. The Council may create laws defining information levels and punishments for leaks.

C. "Impeachment"

I. Impeachment is the process for removing a President, Vice President, or high official for serious misconduct.

II. Impeachment proceedings may begin with support from at least 40% of active Council votes.

III. The accusation must be clearly stated, and the accused official must be given a chance to respond.

IV. Removal from office requires at least 60% support from active Council votes.

V. A removed official may be banned from future office if the Council decides the offense was severe.

D. "Grounds for Impeachment"

I. An official may be impeached for violating this Constitution.

II. An official may be impeached for ignoring Council votes or vetoes.

III. An official may be impeached for leaking confidential or secret information.

IV. An official may be impeached for attempting to cancel elections, dissolve the Council, rule without authority, or make themselves permanent ruler.

V. An official may be impeached for abandoning office or refusing to perform their duties.

Article VIII. Emergencies and Constitutional Amendments

A. "Emergency Powers"

I. The President may use emergency powers only when Vatria faces an immediate threat and waiting for normal approval would seriously harm the Federation.

II. Emergency powers may be used for defense, griefing response, infrastructure protection, diplomatic crisis, or urgent server matters.

III. Emergency actions must be temporary and limited to what is necessary.

IV. The Council may review, cancel, or veto emergency actions.

V. Emergency powers may not be used to cancel elections, change this Constitution, remove Council members permanently, annex or give away territory permanently, or make any person permanent ruler.

B. "Amendments"

I. This Constitution may be amended when necessary.

II. Amendments may be proposed by the President, Vice President, or any active Council member.

III. A proposed amendment must clearly state what it changes.

IV. An amendment passes with at least 60% support from active Council votes.

V. Passed amendments become part of this Constitution and must be publicly recorded.

C. "Protected Principles"

I. No amendment may make one person permanent ruler of Vatria.

II. No amendment may remove presidential elections without creating another fair system.

III. No amendment may give one province more Council votes than another.

IV. No amendment may remove all limits on the President or Council.

V. No amendment may abolish the federal nature of Vatria without an extraordinary process approved by the Council.

D. "Interpretation"

I. If this Constitution is unclear, it shall be interpreted in a way that protects Vatria, its provinces, active citizenship, and democratic government.

II. The Council may interpret unclear parts of this Constitution when necessary.

III. Interpretations may not create powers that clearly contradict the written Constitution.

IV. If an interpretation causes serious disagreement, the Council should clarify the issue through amendment or federal law.

Article IX. Final Principles

A. "Continuity of Government"

I. The government of Vatria shall continue even if officials become inactive, resign, or are removed.

II. Empty offices shall be filled according to succession, election, or federal law.

III. Inactivity shall not be allowed to permanently stop the government.

IV. Temporary actions taken during confusion or crisis must be reviewed once normal government function returns.

B. "Oath of Office"

I. The President, Vice President, Council members, and high officials may be required to swear or accept an oath of office.

II. The oath shall require loyalty to Vatria, respect for the Constitution, and fair service to the Federation.

III. No official shall be required to swear loyalty to another person above the Constitution.

IV. Breaking the oath may be used as evidence in impeachment or removal proceedings.

C. "Closing Principle"

I. Vatria shall be led by its President, protected by its Council, and founded upon its provinces and citizens.

II. The President shall lead.

III. The Council shall restrain.

IV. The provinces shall endure.

V. The Constitution shall stand above all.
